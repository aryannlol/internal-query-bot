# Leave Policy

## 1. Purpose
This policy defines the types of paid and unpaid leave available to employees and establishes the procedures for requesting, approving, tracking, and reporting leave. The objective is to ensure consistent application of leave rules while maintaining business continuity.

---

## 2. Scope
This policy applies to all full-time and part-time employees. Contract workers and interns are governed by the terms specified in their individual agreements.

---

## 3. Types of Leave

### 3.1 Annual Leave (Vacation)
- Entitlement: 20 days per calendar year
- Leave is pro-rated for employees joining during the year
- Annual leave is intended for rest and personal time away from work

### 3.2 Sick Leave
- Entitlement: 10 days per calendar year
- Sick leave does not accumulate across years
- Medical documentation may be required for extended or repeated absences

### 3.3 Personal / Bereavement Leave
- Entitlement: Up to 5 days per calendar year
- Applicable for personal emergencies or bereavement of immediate family members

### 3.4 Parental Leave
- Entitlement: Up to 18 weeks
- Includes statutory leave plus company-provided top-up where applicable
- Eligibility and pay structure follow applicable legal requirements

### 3.5 Public Holidays
- Observed public holidays are published annually by the HR department
- Employees required to work on public holidays will be compensated according to company guidelines

### 3.6 Unpaid Leave
- Granted on a case-by-case basis
- Subject to business needs and management approval
- Unpaid leave does not accrue benefits during the leave period unless required by law

---

## 4. Accrual and Carry-Over

### 4.1 Accrual
- Annual leave accrues monthly at a rate of 1.67 days per month
- Accrual begins from the employee’s start date

### 4.2 Carry-Over
- Maximum carry-over into the following calendar year is 10 days
- Carried-over leave must be used by June 30 of the following year
- Unused carried-over leave after this date will be forfeited

---

## 5. Leave Request and Application Process

### 5.1 Standard Leave Requests
- Leave requests must be submitted through the HR system
- Requests must be submitted at least 14 calendar days in advance
- The request must include:
  - Leave type
  - Start and end dates
  - Reason for leave (where applicable)

### 5.2 Sick and Emergency Leave
- Sick or emergency leave must be reported by 9:00 AM on the first day of absence
- Notification must be provided to both the direct manager and the HR system
- Medical documentation may be requested for absences exceeding two consecutive working days

---

## 6. Approval Authority

### 6.1 Standard Approval Levels
- Up to 10 consecutive working days: Direct Manager approval
- 11 or more consecutive working days: Department Director approval

### 6.2 Special Approval Conditions
- Leave during peak operational periods (e.g., quarter-end close, major product launches) requires:
  - Department Director approval
  - Minimum 30 days’ advance notice
- Exceptions to approval limits require Executive approval

---

## 7. Tracking and Reporting

- All leave is recorded and tracked in the HR system
- Employees are responsible for reviewing their leave balances at least once per quarter
- Negative leave balances are not permitted except under exceptional circumstances approved by Executive management

---

## 8. Compliance and Disciplinary Action

- Failure to follow the prescribed leave notification or approval process may result in:
  - Leave being recorded as unpaid
  - Disciplinary action in accordance with company policy
- Repeated violations may be escalated to HR and senior management

---

## 9. Policy Review
This policy is reviewed annually by the HR department and may be updated to reflect changes in business needs or legal requirements.

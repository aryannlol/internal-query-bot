# Procurement Policy

## Purpose
This policy governs the acquisition of goods and services for the company.

## Purchase Request Process
- Employee submits purchase request via the procurement system.
- Request includes business justification, estimated cost, and preferred vendor (if applicable).
- Request routes to appropriate approvers based on amount and category.

## Approval Authority
- **Up to $2,500**: Direct manager
- **$2,501–$10,000**: Department Director
- **$10,001–$50,000**: Finance Director
- **Over $50,000**: Executive team (CEO + CFO)

## Approval Thresholds by Amount
- **$0–$500**: Manager may approve without quotes
- **$501–$5,000**: Minimum 2 competitive quotes required
- **Over $5,000**: Formal RFP or 3+ competitive bids required

## Vendor Selection Rules
- Selection must be based on best overall value (price, quality, delivery, service).
- Preferred vendors on approved list must be used when available.
- Conflicts of interest must be disclosed.
- No purchases from employees, family members, or related parties without Executive approval.

## Emergency Procurement Handling
- Emergency purchases (immediate need to prevent significant business harm) may bypass standard quote/bid requirements.
- Emergency procurement must be approved by Department Director or higher.
- Justification and documentation required within 5 business days after purchase.
- All emergency procurements are subject to post-review by Finance.

All purchases must comply with this policy. Unauthorized purchases are not reimbursable.

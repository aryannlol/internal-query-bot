# Incident Management Policy

## Purpose
This policy establishes the process for identifying, responding to, and learning from security and operational incidents.

## What Qualifies as an Incident
An incident is any event that:

- Compromises or threatens the confidentiality, integrity, or availability of company systems/data
- Violates company policy or applicable law
- Causes or may cause material disruption to business operations

## Notification Requirements
All employees must report suspected incidents immediately to:

- IT Security team (primary channel)
- Direct manager (secondary)

## Incident Severity Levels

- **Critical (Severity 1)**: Immediate threat to business operations or data breach affecting customers
- **High (Severity 2)**: Significant impact, potential regulatory violation
- **Medium (Severity 3)**: Limited scope, manageable within normal operations
- **Low (Severity 4)**: Minor impact, routine handling

## Approval Authority During Incidents

- **Severity 1 & 2**: CISO or designated Incident Commander has authority to take immediate containment actions
- **Severity 3 & 4**: Department Head or IT Manager may authorize response actions

## Post-Incident Review Process

- All incidents rated Severity 2 or higher require a formal post-incident review.
- Review completed within 10 business days of incident closure.
- Review team includes at minimum: Incident Commander, affected department lead, IT Security.
- Report documents root cause, timeline, response effectiveness, and lessons learned.
- Action items assigned with owners and deadlines.

All incidents must be documented in the company incident tracking system.
